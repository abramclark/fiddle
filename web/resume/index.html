About Me

My answer to "what're you going to be when you grow up" started out as "inventor", then later at age 6 became "scientist". In middle school I explored books and many articles on philosophy, microbiology, and subatomic physics. In my early teens, realizing real scientific research is extremely slow and tedious, I developed a strong interest in consciousness and artificial intelligence, complimented by an enthusiastic pursuit of programming which I started in 5th grade.

In my early 20s it became clear to me that strong AI, if achieved at all, would likely result in a totally opaque process that, at best, would be a poor and morally questionable mimic of a human mind. I decided to create a far more useful technology; intuitive software and development tools. As software subsumes more and more of our culture and society, people increasingly rely on software engineers and designers. Along with all engineers, I'm faced with the challenges of working with the increasingly complex tools we are becoming increasingly dependent on.

More importantly, humanity's survival is becoming dependent on us making the right decisions based on a complex systems level understanding of our natural environment and economies. In short, I'm most excited about tools for spreading accurate information, understanding complex systems, and teaching. Building these tools is the most positively impactful thing I can imagine.
